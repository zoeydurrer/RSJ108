An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to become awesome ...

Assembly time: TBD minutes
Awesome rating: 101% awesome

Have you ever wanted to be the next Kylie Jenner? Anything is possible! Follow these 5 simple steps to unlock your inner influencer.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Passion
  • iPhone (might get by with an Android)
  • Creative mind and eye
  • Good lighting
  • Camera (optional)
  • Positive attitude!

  1. Find your niche.

    Pick something you are passionate about and stick to it. Whether it’s fashion, sports, comedy, or wellness, pick something and stick to it. Followers who relate to your niche will be attracted to your page and will keep coming back.

  2. Take high quality photos.

    This one seems obvious. Better photos= better instagram. Use good lighting, pick up a real camera, clean the smudge off the lens. Edit your photos in apps like Lightroom and VSCO. There are a lot of photos on Instagram, so make your followers want to look at yours.

    Little girl holding pink camera to her eye.

  3. Create your brand.

    Treat your Instagram page like a brand. Pick a username people will recognize right away. Pick a color scheme that is cohesive across your page. What’s your aesthetic? Stick to it. If you’re going for something more professional—create a logo and a brand statement. Make it clear who you are and stay consistent with it. People are attracted to what they recognize.

  4. Be original.

    It’s easy to fall in the trap of trends and templates. Use them, but put your own twist to it. Copying trends might get you views at first, but you’ll soon blend in with everyone else. Post content that is truly you, that way your fans are there for the real you. Develop a style that is your own with layouts, filters, and editing styles. Don’t be scared to try something that’s never been done before!

  5. Understand the algorithm.

    Research the times when people are most active on Instagram. Then, post your content when you know followers are scrolling. Respond to your comments for engagement. Use hashtags with intent–relevant and not too much. Tag the location and people relevant in your photos when applicable.
